Neotropical Bamboo Systematics

How many species of bamboo are there in the neotropics? How are they related to each other and other bamboos? These are some of the central questions that drive our bamboo research.

Though much remains to be determined, we know that there are (at least) three main clades of native Neotropical woody bamboo. These align with three previously recognized subtribes: Arthrostylidiinae, Chusqueinae, and Guaduinae (e.g., Tyrrell et al. 2012). There are also two subtribes of "non-woody" (a.k.a. herbaceous) bamboos (cf. Oliveira et al. 2020). The woody Neotropical subtribes all share a common ancestor, but the relationships among woody and herbaceous clades are still under investigation.
The classification of species and genera within and among the woody subtribes has required modification to align the taxa within each subtribe to the group's putative evolutionary history (Clark et al. 2020, Tyrrell et al. 2018). Realigning the classification is a critical step toward forming a stable, and ideally predictive, taxonomy for the New World bamboos. Novel entities continue to be discovered (e.g., Ruiz-Sanchez et al. 2019, Tyrrell and Clark 2013, Fisher et al. 2011, Clark et al. 2007, Judziewicz and Tyrrell 2007) and our phylogenetic hypotheses provide the necessary framework for gauging morphological variability and species circumscriptions.
